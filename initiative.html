Social Initiatives

1. KEEP IT CLEAN KIDS

Kick aims to provide a clean,safe,healthy and sustainable environment for children in two villages of Uttarakhand. Our desire is to protect the modesty of the Girl child. A sensitive,caring atmosphere is our larger Goal. We realise our Dream can be crafted by our own hands. All we need is a Firm Resolve.

Check It out Here kick.net.in

Be the Change

As students studying in metropolitan cities of India, we are sometimes unaware of the condition of the rest of the country. As little as it might sound in the big picture even the smallest of things can make a big difference. In the villages of Ranikhet, Uttarakhand the children are not privileged to stay in a clean environment with hygenic toilets. We saw in the school a playground without a "net", and a very small patch in which only a few could play. The city was charming and simple, however the larger issues were neglected. The largest issue was that of unhygenic toilets. We had to do something.

We have resolved to set up E-toilets in these villages.

2. Social Initiative Project Patang

I started visiting the centre in the 10th grade. My brother and I visited “The Children of the world “ an Orphanage every week and took basic food supplies for these little children. We slowly started building a bond with them and it took us few months to win their trust and friendship. We tried to teach them basic numbers and simple spoken English and the alphabet. Now, most of the children can speak and read in English, solve simple Math sums and all of them know how to be creative with their activities in their spare time. These activities include drawing, Just a minute speaking, basic Scrabble and Chess. All of these techniques were taught by my brother and I did this over a span 3 year of time and is one something that we do week in and week out even now.

Patang Throw your dreams into space like a kite, you never know what it will bring back, New Life, a new friend or a new love. I saw dreams in the eyes of children who live in “The Children of world” an Orphanage. These children had dreams too. Dreams of a family, dreams of a home, dreams of a school & an education & above all a dream of being Loved
